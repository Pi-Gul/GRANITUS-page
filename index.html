1. HYPOTHESIS 9

Prediction

Phonetic errors (SENT_TYPE:PHON) will have more influence on the assessment of the speakers qualities (SPEAKER_PERSONAL_RATING) than grammatical errors (SENT_TYPE:GRAM). This effect will be reduced or absent in native-accented speech (ACCENT).

[The original prediction from OSF did not involve the Personality Dimension (warmth/competence). I added it as a factor to the model because this was an important part of the experiment design (each sentence was always presented twice: with warmth adjectives and competence adjectives). I did not test its influence for now because it would require interpreting a triple interaction (Sentence Type x Accent x Personality Dimension). We can think about doing it later. -PG]

Variables

Personality Rating (SPEAKER_PERSONAL_RATING)

Outcome variable measured in the Perceptual Experiment. Created by averaging the ratings for 4 adjectives describing the speakers personality traits in terms of warmth or competence for each sentence. Scale: 1(negative)-7(positive). Every participant provided 12 ratings (6 warmth, 6 competence) in each of the 6 main experimental conditions (NAT-WF, NAT-GRAM, NAT-PHON, FOR-WF, FOR-GRAM, FOR-PHON), giving 72 datapoints per participant (36 warmth, 36 competence).

Personality Dimension (PERSONAL_DIMENSION)

Type of personality traits rated for a given sentence. Warmth traits (WARMTH), competence traits (COMP).

Sentence Type (SENT_TYPE)

Type of sentence. Correct (WF), with grammatical error (GRAM), with phonetic error (PHON).

Accent (ACCENT)

The accent of the speaker recording a given sentence. Native-accented (NATIVE), foreign-accented (FOREIGN).

Conclusion

Prediction is not confirmed. Phonetic errors had less impact on personality ratings than grammatical errors in both native- and foreign-accented speech. The WF-GRAM contrasts were statistically significant in the Native condition (t(100)=3.157, p=0.0059) and in the Foreign condition (t(100)=2.458, p=0.0412). The WF-PHON contrasts were not significant.
